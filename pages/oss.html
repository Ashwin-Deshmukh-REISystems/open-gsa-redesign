We will continually update this page with our current efforts to move to 100% open source. Please let us know what you think. (Discuss | Edit)

Our current efforts are outlined below:

  1. We are spreading our knowledge of Open Source Software (OSS) when possible with recently being asked to share our policy with all CFO Act agencies. Agencies are considering our policy as they develop theirs.

  2. We submitted to lead a panel at the O'Reilly Open Source Conference in Austin in May. We believe it is important to tell our story from practical policy development and code implementation views.

  3. We are hiring a front-end developer and OSS Evangelist to join the GSA Digital Service team. This new team member will lead OSS implementation across the Agency.

This site outlines GSA's response to the Office of Management and Budget's (OMB's) M-16-21 memo, Federal Source Code Policy: Achieving Efficiency, Transparency, and Innovation through Reusable and Open Source Software.

Our current policy outlines how we are realizing one of our GSA IT Vision elements of "Be Open."

Besides the writing and posting of our policy, we have multiple efforts underway to implement it.

  1. We have a two mini-projects underway: 1.) automation of the OSS inventory to place on gsa.gov/code.json and open.gsa.gov/code.json; and, 2.) automation to review files within repositories for sensitive information before making the repository public and making private repositories that are found to have sensitive content until they can be cleaned.

  2. Agency OSS implementation guidance on this site with instruction on how to make closed source code open.

  3. We are redesigning open.gsa.gov including one of its major components, Code. This is the theme of our upcoming Hackathon.
